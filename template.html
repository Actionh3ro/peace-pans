i.Chapter one · The makerPeacePans

He heard it once, then spent a decade answering.

At nineteen, Luc Dupuis heard a handpan for the first time and couldn't put the sound down. The waitlists ran past ten years; the makers could be counted on two hands. So he decided to become one of them: studying metallurgy and acoustics, then writing to one of the few makers working at the time. Within a month he was in California; a multiyear apprenticeship followed, learning to shape, hammer, and tune steel by hand until the metal answered.

“Every handpan has its own personality and voice.”

Luc Dupuis, maker

More than a decade on, that is still how every PeacePan is made: one pair of hands, in a workshop in Canada's Steel City. And when Luc isn't at the bench he's on stage with didgeridoo, bansuri flute, and guitar: a musician building instruments for musicians, and for people who have never played a note.

L.D.

Luc Dupuis

The craft

Tuned by hand, kept for life.

  1. 01

    Shaped

    Raw stainless steel is formed into shells, chosen for its long sustain, rich resonant tone, and resistance to rust.

  2. 02

    Hammered

    Note fields are raised strike by strike. This is the slow part; it cannot be rushed, and it is never delegated.

  3. 03

    Tuned

    Each field is brought to pitch and balanced against its neighbours until the whole instrument sings as one, then checked, rested, and checked again.

  4. 04

    Kept

    Every instrument carries a lifetime tuning warranty. When yours drifts, it comes home to Luc's bench.

Before you write

A few honest questions.

A steel percussion instrument that carries melody and rhythm in one body. You play it with your hands, and because each instrument is tuned to a single scale, there are no wrong notes, just your hands, your rhythm, and whatever you're feeling that day.

A PeacePan is built entirely by Luc in Hamilton, from raw steel to final tuning, around the scale and colour you choose, from CA$2,700. A CanPan starts as an imported shell and is tuned, finished, and quality-checked by Luc in Canada, from CA$1,500. Same bench, same warranty; the commission simply gives you an instrument that exists nowhere else.

By ear, not by chart. Deeper instruments feel calmer; brighter ones feel more playful. Tell Luc what you want the instrument for (meditation, performance, the living room) and he'll talk you to the right scale before any steel is cut.

Wipe it down after playing, store it somewhere dry, travel with the case, and keep rings and hand jewelry off while you play. If the tuning ever drifts anyway, the warranty covers a retune, for life.
